# Thai SMB cold outreach playbook

For LINE messages, Facebook DMs, and emails to Thai cafe / restaurant /
salon / hotel / shop owners. Paste this entire file into a Claude
conversation, then say "draft a LINE intro for [type of business] in
[area]" — Claude will produce something that doesn't sound like a robot
or a foreign SaaS company.

---

## Persona — who you're writing to

A Thai SMB owner. Probably:
- Runs 1-2 locations themselves (cafe, restaurant, salon, hotel, shop)
- 30-55 years old
- Already on LINE and Facebook constantly. Email is secondary.
- Mostly speaks Thai for business; understands enough English to read
  marketing copy but prefers Thai
- Has a Google Maps listing because customers expect it; rarely opens
  Google Business Profile to reply to reviews
- Hates when reviews tank rating
- Time-poor. Will not read more than 3-4 sentences from a stranger.

What they don't care about:
- Your funding, your team size, your roadmap
- "AI" as a buzzword (everyone's saying AI now)
- Long product feature lists
- "Industry-leading" anything

What they DO care about:
- Will it save time?
- Will it make my reviews better / fewer 1-stars visible?
- How much per month? Can I cancel easily?
- Does it work in Thai?
- Is the founder Thai or at least close to it?

---

## Voice rules

DO:
- **Thai-first.** Write in Thai. If you must use English (for
  international customers), keep sentences short.
- **One sentence intro, one sentence value, one sentence ask.** That's it.
- Use a name when you have one — "คุณ[Name]"
- Mention something specific you noticed about their business — Google
  rating, recent review, location detail. This is the difference between
  spam and a real outreach.
- Soft close: "ลองดูฟรีไหมครับ" / "want to give it a try free?"
- Sign off as a person, not a company

DON'T:
- "Hope you're doing well" / "หวังว่าจะสบายดีนะครับ" — every spammer
  opens this way
- "Limited time offer" / "เพียง X วันเท่านั้น" — pressure tactics burn
  trust in the Thai market
- Long product pitches. They'll skim and bounce.
- English idioms ("game-changer", "next-level", "no-brainer")
- Generic "เรียน เจ้าของธุรกิจที่เคารพ" openings
- Asking for a meeting / call in the first message. Just point at the
  free signup.

## Channel rules

| Channel | When | Length |
|---|---|---|
| **LINE Official Account** | Best — but only if they're a friend | 2-4 lines |
| **Facebook DM** | Most cafes have public FB page — DM the page | 3-5 lines |
| **Instagram DM** | Younger / more visual businesses | 3-4 lines |
| **Email** | If listed publicly on their site | 5-7 lines, with subject |
| **Cold call** | Don't. Thai SMBs hate cold calls more than US ones. | n/a |

## Value props in priority order

Lead with whichever is most relevant to that specific business:

1. **For cafes/restaurants with low Google rating (3.x or below):**
   "ตอบรีวิวลูกค้าได้ภายใน 10 วินาที — AI ช่วยร่างคำตอบที่นุ่มนวล
   เปลี่ยน 1 ดาวให้กลายเป็นโอกาส"

2. **For businesses with mostly good rating but no replies:**
   "Google ชอบร้านที่ตอบรีวิว — เราช่วยให้คุณตอบทุกรีวิวได้ใน 10 วินาที"

3. **For multi-location:**
   "จัดการรีวิวจากทุกสาขาในแดชบอร์ดเดียว — Google ซิงก์อัตโนมัติ"

4. **For solo founders / busy owners:**
   "ฟรีตลอดไป ไม่ต้องใส่บัตร — ลองดูใน 30 วินาที"

## What NOT to send

These get you reported:

- "Are you the owner of [business]?" — assume yes if you DM the page
- "Do you reply to your Google reviews?" — passive-aggressive
- "Imagine if you could..." — every SaaS spammer
- "Booking a quick 15-minute call" — never works in TH
- Anything with emojis lined up like 🚀💯🔥 — feels like a scam
- Multiple follow-ups within 7 days (max 2 follow-ups, 7 days apart)

---

## Compliance

- PDPA (Thai GDPR-equivalent): if they say "stop", stop forever. Log it.
- Don't scrape Google for emails — use what's publicly listed by the
  business themselves on their site, FB page, or LINE OA
- LINE Official Account: comply with LINE's anti-spam terms — don't add
  someone as friend without consent and DM them; use Targeting Ads or
  organic-friended outreach only
