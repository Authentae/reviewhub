This is extortion, and it's against Google's policies. Don't engage privately, don't pay. Build a paper trail and report.

RED FLAGS
  • Vague review with no specific incident details
  • Private message offering to "remove the review" for a refund, free night, or comp
  • Reviewer has a thin profile with similar tactics on other businesses
  • The "stay" date doesn't match any booking in your PMS
WHAT TO DO
  • Screenshot the review, the profile, and any private messages — timestamps matter
  • Report via Google Business Profile → Reviews → flag → "Conflict of interest / extortion"
  • In Thailand, you can also report via LINE Channel's official business support if the threat came through LINE
  • Reply publicly using the COMPETITOR/SERIAL template below — brief, factual, never engage the demand

The four 1-stars, at a glance.

01
LEGITIMATE · SPECIFIC

Specific acknowledgment + concrete action.

Name the issue. Name the fix. Name the date. Don't apologize for things you didn't do.

02
LEGITIMATE · PATTERN

Acknowledge the pattern, take it offline.

Don't deny it in public. Don't promise a fix you can't ship. Invite a direct conversation.

03
COMPETITOR / SERIAL

Brief, polite, factual. Then flag.

One sentence, no engagement with the false claim. Then report it through Google's flow.

04
EXTORTION POSSIBLE

Document everything. Then evaluate.

Screenshot, save messages, report. Don't reply privately to demands. Reply publicly in template 03.
