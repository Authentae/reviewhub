# Wave 5 customer-dev question packs — by vertical

When a Wave 5 prospect replies (Outcome A, D, or E from the outcomes
tree), use these vertical-specific question packs. The opener
(`docs/wave-postmortems/wave-5-followup-template.md`) drove engagement;
this pack steers the conversation toward usable signal.

**Format:** works for both async email back-and-forth (Earth's default
per `about_me_observed.md` — no calls) AND for the rare scheduled call
(Outcome E). Each pack lists 1 opener + 5 core questions + listen-for
cues + closing ask.

**Pre-conversation prep (5 min, ALL verticals):**

1. Open their Google Maps page in a tab. Read the 3 most recent 5-star
   reviews + the 3 most recent <5-star reviews.
2. Note ONE specific name / treatment / dish / fighter that appears
   in multiple 5-star reviews → that's the prospect's "voice" anchor.
3. Note the response pattern: do they reply to reviews? Generic? Personal?
   None at all? This tells you what gap ReviewHub fills for THEM.

## Muay Thai pack (Chuwattana / Eminent Air / Luktupfah / Rithirit / Master Toddy)

**Why muay thai is different:** gyms have foreign-vs-local guest mix,
fighters reviewed BY NAME (trainers, not just gym brand), and the
revenue model is repeat foreign customers paying ~$30-100/session for
multi-week stays. A 5-star review tagging "Kru Daeng" is worth 5
generic ones because foreign fighters research trainers by name.

### Opener (1 line, async or call)

> "Quick context for me: what's the rough split between local Thai
> students and foreign fighters at your gym? I'm trying to see
> whether the drafts I'm making would feel right for your usual
> reviewers."

**Why:** disqualifies non-fit politely. If they say "90% Thai locals,
short sessions" — they're NOT a fit for English-default ReviewHub.

### 5 core questions

1. **Voice fit:** "When you read the {trainer-name-from-reviews} draft
   I sent, did it sound like something you (or your trainer) would
   actually type? Or did it feel like AI?"
   - *Listen for:* specific words they'd change → that's the "voice"
     they want preserved. If "yes, sounds right" → they trust the
     drafts; move to pricing question.

2. **Time cost today:** "How long does it usually take you to reply to
   a Google review? Or do you skip them?"
   - *Listen for:* time = pain. If "I don't reply" → ReviewHub becomes
     their reply-from-zero tool. If "30 min per review" → it's a
     time-saver, anchor pricing against time saved.

3. **Reply mix:** "Do you reply in English when the review is in
   English, and Thai when it's in Thai? Or one default language?"
   - *Listen for:* multilingual prep work. ReviewHub auto-handles
     this; bring it up only if they say "yes, that's a hassle."

4. **Trust anchor:** "What's the ONE thing in a reply that would
   make a fighter coming back next year remember the gym? A specific
   trainer's name? The story their review told? Something else?"
   - *Listen for:* their answer becomes the drafting prompt's
     personalisation seed.

5. **Decision-maker:** "When you decide whether to try a new tool
   for the gym, what makes you say yes? Free trial? Seeing it work
   on someone else's reviews? Talking to the founder?"
   - *Listen for:* what would close them. Not pitch — diagnose the
     close mechanism.

### Closing ask (Outcome E / paying-customer path)

> "Want to try the free tier for 30 days? You connect Google once,
> drafts arrive on LINE every new review, you copy + paste into
> Google. If after 30 days you're not paying, I delete your data
> and we part ways. No card needed to start. Sound good?"

## Spa pack (CORAN / Dahra / Infinity / Preme / Treasure)

**Why spa is different:** therapists named in reviews matter more than
the brand. Premium positioning means the reply tone matters
(over-formal kills it; too casual kills it). Foreign / hotel-guest
mix varies wildly between Sukhumvit (Thonglo) spas and Silom spas.

### Opener (1 line)

> "Curious — when a 5-star review names a specific therapist (like
> the {therapist-name} pattern in your recent reviews), do you
> usually mention that therapist back in your reply, or keep it
> generic?"

**Why:** answers tell you whether they CARE about the personalisation
that's ReviewHub's whole pitch. If "we keep it generic" → they're
either OK with bland (not your customer) or haven't realised the
better way (your customer, ready to be convinced).

### 5 core questions

1. **Reply quality bar:** "When I sent the draft for the {treatment-
   name} review, did the tone feel right for a {boutique/premium}
   spa, or was it too {casual/formal/whatever-they-flag}?"

2. **Treatment-specific calls:** "Does mentioning a specific
   treatment in the reply (like 'thanks for choosing the {signature
   treatment}') feel natural, or does it read salesy?"
   - *Listen for:* whether they ALREADY do this manually. ReviewHub
     just makes it consistent. If they don't → they're the customer.

3. **Volume:** "Roughly how many reviews per week does the spa get?
   And do you reply to all of them or just the obviously good or
   obviously bad ones?"
   - *Listen for:* volume × reply ratio = current pain. If 20/week
     and they reply to 5, ReviewHub closes the 15-reply gap. Easy
     ROI math.

4. **Channel:** "Where do you usually see a new review notification?
   Google Business app, email, your manager flags it to you?
   Something else?"
   - *Listen for:* if Google app → LINE / Telegram is faster. If
     email → ReviewHub-on-LINE is the upgrade. If "my manager"
     → maybe they're not the buyer; ask who is.

5. **Pricing anchor:** "If a tool drafted every reply for you in
   your voice, and you just hit copy-paste in Google, what would
   that be worth per month at this volume?"
   - *Listen for:* their number. NOT to negotiate — to learn the
     range. If 5 spas say ฿1500/mo (~$40), the $14 anchor is too
     low; raise. If they say "free" → fundamental fit question.

### Closing ask

> "30-day free trial — you connect Google, drafts ping on LINE,
> you decide every reply (copy + paste into Google). If after a
> month you're not seeing time saved, walk away. No card to start."

## Coffee pack (Ink and Lion / Ceresia / + future opens)

**Why coffee is different:** the bean-geek crowd writes reviews
ABOUT the coffee origin / brew method, not just service. Replies
that engage the geek-detail signal "I get you." The WFH-laptop
crowd writes about ambience and ROI is lower per-customer. Different
customer types within the same shop.

### Opener (1 line)

> "Looking at your reviews — they seem split between bean-geek
> regulars (single-origin, brew-method nerds) and the WFH-laptop
> crowd. When you reply, do you usually engage the bean-geek
> details, or keep it neutral so both feel welcome?"

### 5 core questions

1. **Customer-type voice:** "Should the draft for a bean-geek review
   sound different from the draft for a WFH-comfort review? Or are
   you fine with one voice that handles both?"
   - *Listen for:* sophistication. If they care → they're the high-
     value customer.

2. **Owner vs barista voice:** "Right now if a review is replied to,
   is that you or a barista on shift? Should the drafts sound like
   the OWNER (you) or like a STAFF voice?"
   - *Listen for:* who's the buyer. If "the barista handles it" →
     you might be selling to the wrong person.

3. **Local vs tourist:** "What's the rough mix of local regulars
   vs first-time tourist visits in your reviews?"
   - *Listen for:* if mostly tourist → reply quality matters less
     (they're not coming back). If mostly regulars → ReviewHub's
     personalisation matters more.

4. **Recent gap:** "Looking at your last 10 reviews, how many got
   a reply? How many of the unreplied are you OK with vs wish you
   had replied to?"
   - *Listen for:* the "wish I had" count = the gap ReviewHub fills.

5. **Tooling currently:** "Are you already using anything for review
   replies — even a Notion template, a Google Sheet of canned
   responses, anything? Or fully manual?"
   - *Listen for:* if "Notion template" → they've ALREADY identified
     the pain; ReviewHub is the upgrade. If "fully manual" →
     diagnose whether they want the upgrade or are happy manual.

### Closing ask

Same as muay thai — 30-day free, no card.

## Dental pack (Asok Montri / IDENT / future opens)

**Why dental is different:** PHI / patient-privacy concerns are
REAL. A 5-star review naming "Dr. Bee" is fine to acknowledge; a
review describing "the implant surgery I had Tuesday" requires the
reply to NOT confirm any treatment detail (HIPAA-equivalent
concerns vary by country, but legal anxiety is universal).
Per-review revenue impact is highest of any vertical — one lost
patient = $500-3000.

### Opener (1 line)

> "Sensitive question first: are there any treatment details
> patients mention in their reviews that you specifically AVOID
> repeating in your reply? Want to make sure the drafts respect
> that boundary."

**Why:** this is the dental-specific concern. If you don't ask, they
might assume the AI will leak. Asking proactively builds trust faster
than any pitch slide.

### 5 core questions

1. **Privacy boundary:** "If a review says 'Dr. Smith fixed my root
   canal,' is it OK for the reply to thank them, or does even
   acknowledging the procedure cross the line for you?"
   - *Listen for:* their privacy line. Will inform the draft
     prompt's avoid-list.

2. **Reply tone:** "Does the reply tone need to feel medical-
   professional, friendly-clinic, or something in between?"
   - *Listen for:* if "absolutely professional" → ReviewHub's
     warm-bistro tone might miss. Adjust the prompt.

3. **English vs Thai patients:** "What's the mix between English-
   speaking international patients and Thai patients? Should drafts
   default to the review's language, or always English?"
   - *Listen for:* most foreign-patient-heavy clinics want
     consistency in English. ReviewHub auto-matches; explain only
     if asked.

4. **Decision-makers:** "Who decides whether the clinic adopts a
   new tool — you alone, you + the office manager, or do you
   need to run it past partners?"
   - *Listen for:* if multi-stakeholder, qualify accordingly.
     Multi-stakeholder dental deals are slower; that's fine.

5. **Pain signal:** "When a patient leaves a 5-star review and
   you don't reply, do you feel like you missed a chance, or
   is that just normal operating reality?"
   - *Listen for:* if "feels like a miss" → ReviewHub's value is
     emotional + reputational, anchor on that. If "just reality"
     → harder sell; pivot to efficiency angle.

### Closing ask

> "30-day free trial, you connect the Google Business Profile,
> drafts arrive privately on LINE, YOU read every one before
> anything goes public. If after 30 days the boundary isn't
> being respected or you're not seeing time saved, walk away.
> No card, no commitment."

## What this pack is NOT for

- **Cold prospects who haven't engaged.** Use `docs/outreach/wave-5-drafts.md`
  for cold, this for engaged.
- **Closed-won customers post-purchase.** Use the first-customer
  playbook in `docs/skills/first-customer-playbook.md`.
- **Outcomes A or D (immediate paid reply / not-interested).**
  Those have their own action paths in `wave-5-outcomes-tree.md`.

## Use pattern

Async (default per Earth's preference):
1. Prospect replies to followup OR cold-opens via LINE/email
2. Earth reads their reply, identifies vertical, picks pack
3. Sends 1-2 questions per turn (not all 5 at once)
4. Listens for the cues
5. Closes when the cues align

Call (rare — Outcome E only):
1. Schedule within 24h, 20 min cap
2. Earth reads the pack 5 min before
3. Asks all 5 in flow (skip questions whose answers are obvious from
   conversation)
4. Closes within the 20 min
