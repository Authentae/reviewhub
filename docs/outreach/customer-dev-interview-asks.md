## What success looks like

- **1 reply yes** = enormous win. Whatever they say, you learn more from
  that one conversation than from 50 more cold prospects. They become
  the design partner for the next phase.
- **0 replies yes** = also a real signal. Means even engaged prospects
  won't pay for their attention. The bottleneck is even earlier than
  the audit page — it's the value-prop framing itself.
- **A "no" with a sentence of why** = the best possible outcome short
  of yes. That sentence shapes everything.

## Cost

- 6 × 1,500 THB = 9,000 THB (~$260) IF all 6 accept
- More realistic: 1-2 accept → 1,500-3,000 THB (~$45-90)

That's literally the cheapest way to find out why nothing converts.

## Logistics

- Lazada vouchers: buy at lazada.co.th/wallet — denominations of 100,
  500, 1000 THB available. 3× 500 THB = 1,500 voucher code.
- Send code via email reply IMMEDIATELY after their "ok" — good faith
  matters more than survey-style stinginess
- Then ask questions one at a time. Don't dump all 3 in one message.
- Save answers verbatim in `docs/customer-dev-notes-YYYY-MM-DD.md` —
  these are the most valuable data points the project will ever have.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- ❌ Asking the questions all at once → reads as survey, lower response
- ❌ Replying with "great point! By the way, have you considered..." →
   the moment you pitch, they shut down. Listen only.
- ❌ Sending voucher AFTER the answers → looks like bait-and-switch
- ❌ Following up if they don't respond → you said "no follow-up,"
   keep that promise. Their silence is also data.
