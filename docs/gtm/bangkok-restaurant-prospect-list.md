# Bangkok Restaurant Prospect List — How to Build It

I'm not going to fabricate 50 restaurant names — you'd reach out to ghosts. Instead, here's the **fastest method** to build a real, current list yourself in 60-90 minutes, plus a tracking template.

---

## 1. Source: Google Maps + a spreadsheet

This works better than buying a list because every entry comes with the prospect's exact Google Business URL — which is what you need anyway for the /audit page.

### Search queries to run on Google Maps (in order)

For each query, scroll the results and note businesses with **50+ reviews** (review-volume is the qualifying filter):

```
cafe sukhumvit
cafe thonglor
cafe ari
cafe phrom phong
cafe ekkamai
cafe sathorn
restaurant bangkok independent
italian restaurant bangkok
japanese restaurant bangkok independent
french restaurant bangkok
thai fine dining bangkok
brunch bangkok
specialty coffee bangkok
dental clinic sukhumvit
aesthetic clinic bangkok
spa thonglor
spa silom
boutique hotel bangkok
serviced apartment ari
```

Each query yields 20-50 candidates. Pick the top 10 with 50-500 reviews per query → ~150-200 prospects in two hours.

**Skip filters**:
- Anything with "Starbucks", "Coffee Bean", "MK", "Sizzler", "S&P", "Black Canyon" in the name (chain — HQ handles reviews)
- Anything with <20 reviews (too early)
- Anything with >2,000 reviews (likely already on a paid review-management platform like Trustpilot or Birdeye)

**Sweet spot**: 50-500 Google reviews. They feel review pressure but haven't hired anyone.

**How to fill columns fast**:

- **Google URL**: from Google Maps → Share → Copy link. Paste into the spreadsheet.
- **# Reviews + Avg ★**: visible on the Maps listing. Snapshot at time of prospecting — don't update later.
- **IG Handle**: Google "[business name] instagram bangkok" — first result. Or look at their Maps photos: half post their IG handle in captions.
- **LINE / FB**: optional, IG is usually enough.

## 3. Daily prospecting rhythm

**Morning block (45 min)**: Add 10 new prospects to the sheet.
**Mid-day block (45 min)**: Send DMs to 30 prospects (mix new + yesterday's). Use scripts 1, 3, 7 from `thai-cold-dm-scripts.md`.
**End-of-day (15 min)**: Update the response column. Mark non-responders for day-3 follow-up (script 5).

Repeat for 14 days. Realistic outcome:

- 420 DMs sent
- ~60-80 responses (15-20% reply rate is typical for personalized cold DMs)
- ~30 audit requests
- ~5-8 paid trials by end of week 4

If your reply rate is <8% after 100 DMs, your script or targeting is wrong — pivot before sending the next 100.

---

## 4. Neighborhoods to prioritize (in order)

1. **Thonglor / Ekkamai** — highest density of independent F&B + foreigner-friendly owners → most likely to reply in English first
2. **Ari / Phrom Phong** — younger café scene, very review-conscious
3. **Sathorn / Silom** — corporate cafes + lunch spots; review volume is mature
4. **Phra Khanong / On Nut** — upcoming, owners hungrier for tools
5. **Chinatown (Yaowarat) / Phra Nakhon** — older establishments; harder to reach via IG, try Facebook Page or LINE OA
6. **Outside Bangkok** — Chiang Mai (Nimmanhaemin), Phuket (Patong), Hua Hin — only after you exhaust BKK

---

## 5. Beyond F&B (week 3+)

Once you've cycled through F&B, expand to:

- **Aesthetic / dental clinics** — Sukhumvit / Thonglor — review reputation = trust = revenue. Highest LTV segment.
- **Boutique hotels (12-40 rooms)** — Riverside, Phra Nakhon, Old Town. TripAdvisor + Google + Booking.com volume.
- **Co-working spaces** — Ari, Sathorn, Thonglor.
- **Pet groomers / vets** — Bangkok-wide. Underserved by review tools, owner-operated.
- **Fitness studios / yoga / Muay Thai gyms** — review-driven discovery.

---

## 6. What to do if a prospect replies "yes, send me the audit"

1. They submit the form at /audit (or send the URL via DM)
2. You get an email at ADMIN_EMAIL with the lead
3. Reply within **6 hours** if at all possible — speed wins more than polish
4. Spend 20-30 min hand-crafting 10 replies using the public reply tool at /tools/review-reply-generator (free, you've already built it)
5. Send back a single email or PDF with all 10 replies, ending with: *"Want this automated forever — every new review answered in your voice in 10 seconds? Start free at reviewhub.review/register"*
6. Add them to the spreadsheet "Audit Sent: Y" column with timestamp
7. **Day 3**: send a single follow-up if they haven't started a trial: *"Did the audit help? Happy to chat if you have questions."*

Anything past day 7 with no trial = mark "lost" and move on.
