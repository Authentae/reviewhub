# X / Twitter — first-week post drafts (@reviewhubreview)

Drafted 2026-05-07. Applies `docs/skills/social-presence.md` (post
criteria, pre-post checklist) + `docs/skills/content-writing.md`
(brand voice, honesty gate). Earth reviews + approves before any
post goes live.

Rules carried into every draft below:
- Brand voice (no "I"/"me"/"our founder")
- No fake numbers, no testimonials, no team-cosplay
- Web-only positioning (no extension/iOS/Android references)
- Under 280 chars
- Honest stage signal (pre-revenue, founder-built)
- Link unfurls correctly (og-image.png is live; verified 2026-05-07)

---

## Post 1 — pinned introduction (Type D)

The pinned tweet has to answer "what is this account?" before
anything else. Type D (product mention) is the right call for a
first post.

> Hi. We're ReviewHub.
>
> AI drafts a reply to every Google review in the owner's voice.
> You approve. We post it. 10 seconds per reply instead of 10
> minutes.
>
> Google + 60 other platforms via CSV. Free tier covers 3 drafts
> a month.
>
> Built in Bangkok. reviewhub.review

Char count: ~272. Under 280 ✓. Link unfurls to og-image.png ✓.
No extension/iOS references ✓. No fake numbers ✓.

**After posting:** pin via three-dot menu → "Pin to your profile."

## Post 2 — Type B (counter-take) · day 2

Frames a real opinion most owners get wrong. Builds the brand
as having a point of view.

> "Thank you for your feedback" on a 1-star review reads as
> dismissal.
>
> Specific acknowledgment of what actually went wrong reads as
> accountability.
>
> Same words you'd use in person.

Char count: ~195. ✓. No link — reply-engagement post.

## Post 3 — Type A (specific tip) · day 3

A tactical observation an owner can act on today. ~50% of
posts should be this type going forward.

> If you're a small business with under 100 Google reviews,
> asking on the receipt is the highest-leverage move you can
> make this week.
>
> One QR pointing at your direct review form (not the listing
> page). Cuts the path from 6 taps to 2.

Char count: ~245. ✓. No link — pure value.

## Post 4 — Type C (pattern-from-data) · day 4

Honest pattern observation. NOT a fake testimonial. Frames the
brand as paying attention without claiming customers we don't
have.

> Pattern from this week's audit URLs:
>
> Hotel owners reply 3× more often to long detailed reviews
> than to short 5-stars — even though short 5-stars are the
> easier wins.
>
> Two sentences of acknowledgment. 30 seconds. Done.

Char count: ~240. ✓. The "this week's audit URLs" framing is
honest — it refers to the outbound audits we generate for
prospects, which IS a thing we observe.

## Post 5 — Type A (specific tip, Thai-grounded) · day 5

Bangkok-specific, demonstrates the brand isn't generic-SaaS.

> Bangkok hospitality owners: tourists leave reviews in English
> even at Thai-only properties, because that's what Google
> defaults to in their interface.
>
> Replying in the same language as the review (not the language
> of your business) lifts perceived hospitality. Worth the extra
> seconds.

Char count: ~278. ✓. Tight on the limit — verify before posting.

## Pre-post checklist (run before EACH post)

```
[ ] Voice matches social-presence.md §1 (no jargon, no "we're hiring",
    no off-topic)
[ ] Body text actually contains the body in the composer (verify by
    reading the rendered text BEFORE clicking Post — yesterday's
    URL-only failure was caused by skipping this)
[ ] If link present: og:image renders (we have og-image.png live
    at reviewhub.review/og-image.png)
[ ] Length under 280 chars (X composer shows count)
[ ] No claims that need numbers we don't have
[ ] No mentions of dropped-scope features (extension, iOS, Android)
```

---

## Cadence

- Daily through week 1 (7 posts)
- Switch to 3-4 posts/week from week 2 unless a real product
  ship justifies more
- Don't post for the sake of posting. Empty posts train followers
  to scroll past.
