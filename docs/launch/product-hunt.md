# Product Hunt Launch — ReviewHub

Everything you need to paste into Product Hunt's launch form. Target launch
day: a Tuesday or Wednesday (best for category-leader slots). Avoid Monday
(crowded) and Friday (low traffic).

## Screenshots checklist (5 slots, 1280×800 or 1270×760)

1. **Hero screenshot (most-important)** — a Yelp review page with the ✨
   AI Draft button visible next to a review + the draft dialog open.
   This is what a visitor needs to see in 1 second to "get it."
2. **Multi-platform collage** — 4-up: same button on Yelp, Facebook,
   TripAdvisor, and Trustpilot.
3. **The dashboard** — main reviews feed showing cross-platform data.
4. **The free no-signup tool** — `/tools/review-reply-generator` with a
   sample review + drafted reply visible.
5. **Price card** — clean Free/$14/$59 comparison with permanent-free
   callout highlighted.

## Gallery order strategy

Screenshot 1 is what shows in PH search previews. It *must* show the
product in action, not a marketing page. Do not put the landing page as
screenshot 1 — showing "the magic happening" converts 3-5x better than
showing "the homepage."

## Day-of timeline (00:01 PST launch time)

- **T-24h:** finalize screenshots, upload as draft on PH
- **T-1h:** double-check the draft, rewrite tagline one more time
- **T+0:** launch goes live. Post the maker comment immediately.
- **T+5min:** tweet the launch link (pinned tweet for 24 hours)
- **T+15min:** post to your LinkedIn if you have one
- **T+30min:** post to IndieHackers: "Launched on PH today, here's the
  product"
- **T+1h:** post to r/SideProject and r/SaaS (respect their launch rules)
- **T+4h:** reply to every comment. Even "cool!" — reply with a
  question back to extend the thread.
- **T+8h:** post the free-tool link in r/EntrepreneurRideAlong with
  context, NOT a direct promo
- **T+12h:** share update on Twitter: "currently at #X on PH, thanks
  all"
- **T+24h:** final thank-you tweet, PH launch officially ends

## Don'ts

- Don't beg for upvotes in DMs to strangers. PH's algorithm de-ranks
  this.
- Don't create fake accounts to upvote. They will ban you and it hurts
  real products.
- Don't reply to comments with only a link. Answer the question, then
  add the link.
- Don't argue with a negative comment. Agree with it, offer to fix
  what's valid, move on.

## Post-launch follow-up

- **Day 2-3:** write a "here's what I learned from my PH launch" post
  on IndieHackers + Twitter (content from the launch itself)
- **Day 7:** tweet metrics transparently. "Got 340 upvotes, 84
  extension installs, 12 paid signups." Good or bad, transparency
  builds trust.
- **Day 14:** submit to Hacker News as "Show HN" (different audience,
  fresh content angle)

## Metric targets (honest expectations)

A respectable indie SaaS PH launch looks like:

| Metric | Lower bound | Reasonable | Great |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upvotes | 100 | 300 | 500+ |
| Visits to site | 500 | 1,500 | 4,000+ |
| Extension installs (launch week) | 20 | 80 | 200 |
| Free signups | 5 | 25 | 80 |
| Paid conversions (week 1) | 0-1 | 3-5 | 10+ |

Don't measure the launch by upvote count. Measure by installs + signups.
Product Hunt is a distribution channel, not a popularity contest.
