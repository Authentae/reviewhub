   `docs/cold-email-templates.md`)

## ③ Reply scripts — for when people DM/email asking questions

Pre-written so you don't freeze when the first prospect actually
responds. Lead with curiosity, not pitch.

### "Does this work with [Yelp / Facebook / TripAdvisor]?"

> Right now ReviewHub only pulls from Google — that's the highest-volume
> source for ~95% of Thai businesses. Yelp, Facebook, TripAdvisor,
> and Wongnai integrations are next on the roadmap (next 4–8 weeks).
>
> Question for you: which one would unlock the most value for your
> business? I want to build the next integration based on what
> the first 10 paying customers actually need.

### "Is the Thai language quality good?"

> Honest answer: it's good enough that 80% of replies need only minor
> edits — but I always recommend a quick read-through before sending.
> The drafts are tuned to be polite, specific to the review content,
> and short enough that they don't sound bot-generated.
>
> Best way to judge: try the free tool at https://reviewhub.review/tools/review-reply-generator
> — paste a real review you got, see what it drafts.

### "What if my customers don't write much in their reviews?"

> The AI works best with reviews that have at least one specific detail
> ("the curry was too salty", "waited 30 minutes for our table"). For
> bare 5-star reviews with no text, the response templates are your
> friend — and you can save your favourites in Settings → Response
> Templates so you don't keep retyping.

### "How do I cancel?"

> Settings → Billing → Cancel — takes 1 click, no email required.
> You keep access until the end of your billing period. After that
> your account drops back to the free tier (3 AI drafts/month).
>
> No "are you sure?" pop-ups, no exit interview. If you cancel I'd
> love to know why so I can fix it for the next person, but only if
> you feel like sharing.

### "Is my customer data safe?"

> Short version: yes. We pull reviews via Google's public Business
> Profile API (only your own reviews), reply drafts are generated by
> Anthropic's Claude API which doesn't train on customer data, and
> nothing leaves the system to a third party except payment processing
> (LemonSqueezy, which is the merchant of record so they handle PCI).
>
> Long version: see https://reviewhub.review/privacy

### "Can I get a discount / free trial / lifetime deal?"

> Honest answer: no — but the free tier is genuinely usable for a
> single-location business with under 3 reviews/month, and you can
> cancel a paid plan at any time with no penalty. I'd rather have
> a small product with happy customers than a big one with people
> who feel locked in.

## ⑤ When you hit 5 paying customers

That's the threshold to:
- File for Product Hunt launch (`docs/runbooks/launch-day.md` says NOT day 1)
- Approach Wongnai for partnership (you have proof now)
- Start writing the case-study blog posts

Don't celebrate publicly until then. Quiet 5 paying customers > loud 50
free signups.

## What success looks like at midnight on day 1

Per `docs/runbooks/launch-day.md`:

| Tier | Day 1 Result |
|---|---|
| 🟢 Realistic | 50–200 visitors, 10–30 signups, 1–3 paying |
| 🟡 Stretch | 500+ visitors, 50+ signups, 5+ paying |
| 🔴 Disappointment | <10 visitors → distribution problem, NOT product problem |

If you hit the red tier: **don't pivot, don't rebuild, don't quit.** Talk
to the next 5 people on your prospect list. They will tell you what's
wrong faster than any analytics tool.
