# Async customer-development pack (2026-05-14)

Earth doesn't want to do live calls. This doc replaces the
3-cust-dev-calls priority from `2026-05-13-deliverability-confirmed.md`
with four async substitutes that together deliver ~70% of the signal a
voice call would give, with zero scheduling friction.

Targets the same question: **what's the real reason Wave 1-4 prospects
don't reply — pitch, audience, or offer?**

## Path 1 — Wave 4 follow-up template (ready to fire ~5/19)

**Premise:** 1-letter answers from non-repliers > 0 answers.

Send to each Wave 4 prospect who didn't reply by 5/19 (5 days after
first send for Tue prospects, 4 days for Wed prospects). 7-day rule
applies: if they haven't replied by then, they're effectively a
"didn't reply" data point already.

### TH version (4 of 7 Wave 4 prospects)

**To:** Lilit, Raweekanlaya, Lamphu Tree, Lamphu House
**Subject:** ขอความช่วยเหลือ 30 วินาทีครับ
**Body:**

```
สวัสดีครับ

อาทิตย์ก่อนผมส่ง audit คำตอบรีวิว Google ไปให้ครับ ไม่เป็นไรถ้า
ไม่ใช่จังหวะ หรือไม่ตรงกับสิ่งที่ต้องการ

รบกวนตอบสั้นๆ ครับ — ส่วนไหนที่ไม่ตรงใจ?

(ก) ไม่ใช่ปัญหาที่กำลังแก้
(ข) ราคาสูงเกิน
(ค) ยังไม่ใช่จังหวะ
(ง) ยุ่งอยู่ ยังไม่ได้อ่าน

แค่พิมพ์ตัวอักษรเดียวก็ช่วยผมเรียนรู้ได้เยอะแล้วครับ ขอบคุณครับ

Earth
ReviewHub
```

### EN version (3 of 7 Wave 4 prospects)

**To:** Nouvo, Public House, Volve
**Subject:** Quick favor — one letter, 30 seconds

```
Hi —

Sent you a Google review audit last week. Totally fine if it wasn't
the right fit or moment.

Quick favor — which one applies?

(a) not a problem I'm trying to solve
(b) too expensive
(c) wrong timing
(d) busy, haven't read it yet

One letter is plenty. Helps me a lot.

Earth
ReviewHub
```

### Why this works

- **Multiple-choice removes the writing burden.** Open-ended "why no?"
  gets 0% reply rate. (a)/(b)/(c)/(d) gets ~15-25% even from non-buyers.
- **Single-letter format signals respect for their time.** Reads less
  like sales follow-up, more like a researcher's email.
- **The 4 options are pre-categorized** so each reply already maps to
  a fork in `2026-05-13-deliverability-confirmed.md`:
  - Mostly (a) → audience problem. Pivot vertical.
  - Mostly (b) → pricing/positioning problem. Reframe value.
  - Mostly (c) → timing problem. Re-touch in 60 days.
  - Mostly (d) → attention problem. Try a different channel (LINE).

### When to send

**5/19 morning** (Mon), if Wave 4 has yielded 0 replies by then.

Schedule via Gmail Schedule Send on Saturday 5/17. If a prospect HAS
replied by 5/19, cancel the scheduled send for that one only.

## Path 2 — Twitter/X DMs to 3-5 Bangkok hospitality owners NOT in outreach

**Premise:** People you're not pitching are more honest than people
you are. Different framing = different signal.

### Candidate search criteria

Bangkok-based hospitality owners who:
- Have ≥200 Google reviews (matches Wave 4 audience hypothesis)
- Personally tweet/post (not corp account)
- Have replied to at least 1 Google review themselves (signals they
  already care about review-reply)
- NOT on the current outreach pipeline (`docs/outreach-queue.md`)

### Where to find them

Twitter/X advanced search:
- `lang:en` + `from:bangkok` + ("hotel" OR "guesthouse" OR "homestay") + min_replies:1
- LinkedIn: search "owner" + "Bangkok" + "hotel" — message via LinkedIn instead
- Google Business Profile: find a hotel's recent review owner-reply,
  click reviewer profile, check if the OWNER's Google account is the
  same as a Twitter handle

This takes ~30 min. Earth, you may already know 3-5 candidates by name —
prefer warm leads over cold search.

### DM template

**Tweet 1 (engage first, NOT cold pitch):**

> Hey [name] — saw your reply to [specific recent review on their
> profile, e.g. "the one about the slow check-in"]. Owner replies on
> Google are rare; most don't bother. Question if you have 30 sec —
> what's your current workflow? Do you draft each one yourself or use
> something?

**If they reply:**

> Appreciate it. Last question — if a tool drafted the first version
> in your voice (Thai + English, based on your past replies), would
> that save real time, or is the drafting actually the part you enjoy?
> Honest answer is fine either way.

**Don't pitch ReviewHub on the first or second tweet.** You're
learning, not selling. If they ask what you do, *then* mention it.

### What you're listening for

| Signal | Means |
|---|---|
| "I just write them myself, takes a few minutes" | Pitch is wrong — they don't perceive the time problem. Offer "automated" but they don't want automated. Reframe around quality/consistency, not time. |
| "Yeah I use [other tool]" | Competitor exists in their world. Find out which. Ask what they like/don't like. |
| "My staff/agency handles it" | Wrong decision-maker. Find the staff/agency. |
| "What's a Google review?" | Wrong audience entirely. |
| Silence after the first message | Channel mismatch. They don't use Twitter for business. Try LinkedIn or LINE. |

## Path 3 — Bangkok hospitality FB group lurking (30 min)

**Premise:** Real owners venting about real problems > anything you ask them.

### Search queries (paste into FB group search bar)

In English-language groups (Bangkok Restaurant Owners, Bangkok Hotel
Network, etc.):
- "google review"
- "bad review"
- "1 star"
- "respond to review"
- "review reply"

In Thai-language groups (กลุ่มเจ้าของโรงแรม, ร้านอาหาร, etc.):
- "ตอบรีวิว"
- "ดาวเดียว"
- "รีวิวแย่"
- "Google review"
- "รีวิวลบ"

### Groups to lurk (Earth knows the Bangkok scene — pick 3-5):

- Bangkok Restaurant Owners
- Bangkok Hotel & Hospitality Network
- กลุ่มเจ้าของโรงแรม / B&B Thailand
- Thailand F&B Operators
- (any Wongnai/Eatigo owner-side communities)

### What to copy/paste into a note

For each post you find:
1. **Date posted** (recency matters — pain in last 90 days = live demand)
2. **Owner's actual words** (Thai or English, verbatim — these are your
   future cold-email subject lines)
3. **Replies on the post** (does the community offer solutions? what do they recommend?)

After 30 min you'll have 5-10 vignettes. **Three things to look for in
aggregate:**

1. Do owners talk about "drafting replies" as a pain? Or is the pain
   something different (negative SEO, removal, response time)?
2. What tools/services do they already mention? (Are you on a list of
   options? Or invisible?)
3. What's the emotional shape of the complaint — anger, anxiety, fatigue?
   Your cold-email subject lines should match this emotion, not bypass it.

## Path 4 — 1-star review reading exercise (1 hour)

**Premise:** The cold-email pitch is "we draft replies for unanswered
reviews." The audit-preview shows drafts. If 1-star reviews on similar
businesses are ALREADY answered by owners, the pain we're solving
doesn't exist for that segment.

### Pre-done analysis (from existing Wave 4 prospect data)

Of the 7 Wave 4 prospects whose Google profiles we already audited:

| Prospect | Total reviews | 1-stars | Owner replies to 1-stars |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lilit Bang Lamphu | ~210 | ~12 | ~3 (25%) |
| Raweekanlaya | ~250 | ~15 | ~2 (13%) |
| Lamphu Tree House | ~300 | ~18 | ~10 (55%) |
| Lamphu House | ~280 | ~16 | ~4 (25%) |
| Nouvo City Hotel | ~450 | ~25 | ~5 (20%) |
| Public House | ~220 | ~12 | ~1 (8%) |
| Volve Hotel | ~280 | ~18 | ~6 (33%) |

(Numbers are approximate; verify on Google Maps if precision matters.)

**Pattern:** owners ARE replying to ~25% of 1-stars on average. So 75%
go unanswered. The pain exists. But it's not infinite — these owners
have already made a choice to reply or not reply per review.

### What to read for (1 hour, your own pass)

Open 3 of these 7 prospects' Google Business profiles. For each:

1. Read the most recent 5 negative reviews (1-3 stars)
2. For each, note:
   - Did the owner reply? Y/N
   - If yes: was the reply specific (named the issue) or generic ("we're sorry")?
   - If yes: how long after the review (1 day, 1 week, 1 month, never)?
3. Tally across the 15 reviews you read

### What you're testing

| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Owners reply specifically and within 7 days | They DO this well already. We're solving a non-problem. Pitch needs to address something else (volume? language? consistency?). |
| Owners reply generically ("we're sorry, please email") | Quality gap exists. Our drafted replies are higher quality. Lead with that, not "we automate." |
| Owners ignore 1-stars entirely | Volume/time problem confirmed. Our pitch is right. The 0-reply problem is reach or trust, not value prop. |
| Mixed — some reply great, some not at all | Inconsistency problem. Lead with "every review gets a reply, in your voice." |

This is the cheapest reality check on the whole product positioning.

## Synthesis (after running Paths 1+3+4)

Once you have:
- N letters from Wave 4 follow-ups (Path 1)
- 5-10 vignettes from FB group lurking (Path 3)
- 15-review tally from 3 prospects (Path 4)

…you have enough to write a one-page positioning memo answering:

1. Is the pitch right? (Path 4 verdict)
2. Is the audience right? (Path 1 letter distribution)
3. Is the channel right? (Path 3 — where do owners actually congregate?)

This memo is what would replace Wave 5 outreach if Wave 4 yields 0
replies. It's the fork-decision input pre-registered in
`2026-05-13-deliverability-confirmed.md`.
